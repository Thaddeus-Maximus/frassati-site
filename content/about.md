# Who We Are

We are a group of young men around the Bloomington-Normal, IL area who find inspiration from the life of St. Pier Giorgio Frassati.

We meet weekly to study such texts and books. If this sounds like you, [contact us.](/contact)

# Getting to Know St Frassati

![[Pasted image 20260306121352.png]]

Pier Giorgio Frassati lived vibrantly, filling his short 24 years with athletics (mountain climbing, skiing, fencing, sailing), engineering studies, music, dance, poetry, Dante recitals, languages, opera, theater, art, travel, and photography. He was loud and joyful with friends, reverent in prayer, at home with the poor or elites, and driven by humor, love for God, Church, country, and neighbor—always intentional, never ambitious for worldly gain. His life proves sainthood is for everyone: live extraordinarily in ordinary ways, full of joy and deep connections, earning St. John Paul II’s title “Man of the Eight Beatitudes.”

>“We must never exist, but live!”

- -St Frassati
    

## The Tenets of St Frassati’s Spirituality

### Eucharistic Devotion
![[Picture2.png]]
*Pier Giorgio at a mountain-top Mass*

From age 12, Frassati received daily Communion after begging his mother, making the Eucharist the heart of his life for strength against passions and adversities. He adored late into the night, attended processions and congresses, and even won bets at pool halls to bring friends to adoration. The Eucharist fueled his heroic charity and joy.

>“I urge you with all the strength of my soul to approach the Eucharistic Table as often as possible. Feed on this Bread of the Angels…”

-St Frassati

![[Picture3.png]]
*Pier Giorgio at a Eucharistic procession*
  

### Marian Devotion

Frassati loved Our Lady tenderly: a Dominican tertiary praying the Little Office daily, wearing her medal, consecrating himself at 17, and carrying a prized 15-decade rosary everywhere. He brought flowers to her shrines year-round—even in winter snow—and led the Rosary boldly, reciting it loudly on Turin streets, trains, and mountains inviting others to join in. Mary was woven into every adventure as his constant guide to Christ.

>“Our Lady is worth a whole lot to the church, and we owe everything to her.”

- St Frassati
    

  

>“Wherever there was a celebration in her honor, Pier Giorgio would show up with a bunch of flowers.” - Luciana Frassati

### His Love of St Paul

Frassati carried St. Paul’s “Hymn of Charity” (1 Corinthians 13) as his lifelong focus, meditating on perfect love to anchor his life amid a world lacking peace. He read Paul’s Epistles on trams and streets, calling them “words of eternal life,” and urged friends to discover their refreshing, ennobling power. This devotion fed his extraordinary charity, reducing miseries by heeding Paul’s call.

>“In the world that has gone astray from God, there is no peace; it also lacks charity that is true and perfect love. Were we to heed St. Paul a little more, human miseries would diminish.” - St Frassati

  

### His Three Apostolates

Frassati taught three apostolates: good example—living fully by Christian moral law; charity—comforting the suffering with perfect love, where “the charity of Christ needs us” and the heart beats only for others; persuasion—gently drawing peers away from vice toward God’s path of thorns and roses. All three flow from a fortified spirit through prayer, mercy, and study. Young people especially must live them boldly.

>“There are three distinct apostolates… the apostolate of good example… the apostolate of charity… the apostolate of persuasion…” - St Frassati

![[Picture6.png]]

  

###Dominican Influence

As a Dominican tertiary (Girolamo, his religious name taken after his favorite Dominican Girolamo Savonarola). Frassati integrated the Order’s spirituality of contemplation and action: daily Little Office, deep Eucharistic adoration, and social action. Dominican spirituality shaped his purity, courage, and hidden fire of prayer fueling service. It grounded his zeal in intimate union with Christ.

  

### Vincentian Influence

Through the St. Vincent de Paul Society (joined at 17), Frassati embodied Vincentian mercy: visiting the sick and poor, delivering aid, often sacrificing meals or vacations, treating each with friendship and dignity. He saw Christ in the least (Matt 25:40) and served secretly yet tirelessly. This active charity made his love concrete and sacrificial.

### Summary

Frassati lived the Eight Beatitudes daily through radical charity—the greatest virtue that endures forever—uniting contemplation, action, joy, and service. His example calls us to pray deeply, love boldly, and climb “verso l’alto” toward heaven. St. Pier Giorgio Frassati, pray for us!
